I'm a curious geek! :) I enjoy challenging tasks and research at work, as much as hiking in the Himalayas and voluntographing at my favorite animal shelter. I don't enjoy being ordered around (who does, really?), but two words that best describe me? Cat slave!  
What sort of employee does that make me?  

I read about <i>Ikigai</i> first in a magazine, and then in a book by Albert Liebermann and Hector Garcia. Ikigai is a Japanese concept that guides you closer to your purpose; it's about figuring out what gets (or could get) you <i>bounding</i> out of bed every morning.  

The following chart guides you on your path of discovery.  

<img src="/assets/ikigai.png" align ="middle">   

I have a few tips from my experience with this exercise:
- Pick any section to start with; there's no rule to it. I started with the section on things I love doing.
- List every little thing that you think is relevant to a section. It gets interesting when you hit the intersections!
- Don't beat yourself up if you don't have a clear mission or vocation yet. This is a continuous process! You're likely to have a :bulb: moment when you least expect it!
- Place your chart where you'll see it every day. I would finetune my chart each time I passed by it.
- There's no deadline. You're free to approach this exercise by instinct or logic, but eventually, you'll have to answer the tough questions.  
  For example: 'Can I <i>really</i> get paid for this hobby?', 'How important is world peace to me over a good salary?', and so on.  
  Take all the time you need. 
- '5 Whys' is a classic user research technique and worked well for me. To pinpoint your motivations, delve deeper into every list item you add to the chart.  

The results surprised me! Professionally, I've identified motivating factors that make me go the extra mile. I'm lucky to have volunteering oppportunities that combine my passion for photography and mission to contribute to animal welfare activities.

While you may or may not find your Ikigai, I guarantee you will become more aware of your role in the world around you. 

Hope you enjoy this exercise as much as I did!
