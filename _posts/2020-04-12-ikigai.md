I'm a curious geek! :) I enjoy challenging tasks and research at work, as much as hiking in the Himalayas and voluntographing at my favorite animal shelter. Two words that best describe me though? 'Cat slave'!  
What sort of employee does that make me?  

<img src="/assets/ikigai.png" align ="left">  
I read about <i>Ikigai</i> first in a magazine, and then in a book by Albert Liebermann and Hector Garcia. Ikigai is a Japanese concept that helps you find your purpose, and figure out what <i>gets you out of bed every morning</i>.  

Draw the chart out for yourself to find your Ikigai!  

I have a few tips from my experience with this exercise:
- Pick any section to start with; there's no rule to it. I started with the section on things I love doing.
- Don't beat yourself up if you don't have a clear mission or vocation yet. This is a continuous process! You're likely to have a :bulb: moment when you least expect it!
- Place your chart where you'll see it every day. I would finetune my chart each time I passed by it.
- There's no timeline. You're free to approach this exercise dominantly with your heart or brain, but eventually, you'll have to ask yourself the tough questions. For example: 'Can I <i>really</i> get paid for abc skill?', 'How important is world peace to me over a good salary?', and so on. Take all the time you need. 
- '5 Whys' is a classic user research technique and worked well for me. Delve deeper into every point you make on the chart and ask yourself where it all stems from.  

The results surprised me and helped me understand what I want to do with my time, both professionally and vocationally. Hope you enjoy this exercise as much as I did!
